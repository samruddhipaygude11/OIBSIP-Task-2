Nasa Astronaut Sunita Williams

A Timeline of Sunita's Life

  • 1965 - Born in Euclid, Ohio

  • 1983 - Graduated from Needham High School, Massachusetts

  • 1987 - Received a Bachelor of Science degree in physical science from the United States Naval Academy

  • 1987 - Commissioned an ensign in the United States Navy

  • 1989 - Designated a Naval Aviator

  • 1993 - Began training at the U.S. Naval Test Pilot School

  • 1995 - Received a Master of Science degree in Engineering Management from Florida Institute of Technology

  • 1998 - Deployed on USS Saipan and selected by NASA for the astronaut program

  • 2007 - Launched to the International Space Station (ISS) to join the Expedition 14 crew

  • 2012 - Lauched for a four-month stay at the ISS as part of Expedition 32/33

  • 2012 - Served as commander of the ISS for Expedition 33

  • 2015 - Started working with Boeing and SpaceX training commercial spaceflight crews

NASA Experience: Selected as an astronaut by NASA in June 1998, she reported for training in August 1998. Astronaut Candidate Training included orientation briefings and tours, numerous scientific and technical briefings, intensive instruction in shuttle and International Space Station systems, physiological training and ground school to prepare for T-38 flight training, as well as learning water and wilderness survival techniques. Following a period of training and evaluation, Williams worked in Moscow with the Russian Space Agency on the Russian contribution to the space station and with the first Expedition Crew. Following the return of Expedition 1, Williams worked within the Robotics branch on the station’s Robotic Arm and the follow-on Special Purpose Dexterous Manipulator. As a NEEMO2 crew member, she lived underwater in the Aquarius habitat for 9 days. After her first flight, she served as Deputy Chief of the Astronaut Office. She then supported a long-duration mission as Flight Engineer for Expedition 32 and International Space Station Commander for Expedition 33. Williams has spent a total of 322 days in space on two missions; she ranks second on the all-time U.S. endurance list for females. With 50 hours and 40 minutes, she is second on the list of total cumulative spacewalk time by a female astronaut. She is currently training for NASA's Boeing Crew Flight Test mission – the first flight with astronauts on Boeing's CST-100 Starliner.
